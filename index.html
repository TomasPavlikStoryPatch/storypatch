The Problem in Publishing

Children's publishing is broken at the border.

Every year, millions of children's board books go unsold — not because stories fail, but because language locks them to a single market. Publishers must commit to language-specific print runs months before knowing demand. The economics are brutal.

Immigrant families, bilingual households, and linguistic minorities in every country are systematically underserved. The bookshelves are empty — not from lack of stories, but from lack of a better system.

📦
Inventory locked to one language
Unsold stock in Germany cannot be redistributed to Spain. Every market requires a separate print run — a separate financial bet.
💸
Localization costs prohibit testing
Minimum print quantities make it financially unviable to test minority language markets — even when the audience clearly exists.
🗺️
Multilingual families are invisible
Parents who read in one language, children in another — this is the reality of millions of families worldwide. Publishing has never solved it.
⬛
Accessible reading is still an afterthought
A blind parent reading to their sighted child — or vice versa — has virtually no dedicated book format that serves both simultaneously.

The Innovation · UZ CZ 39118

One print run.
Every language market.

StoryPatch introduces a patented text-layer system for children's board books. The book is printed once, in a base language. Removable, interchangeable text overlays — stickers, magnetic labels, or transparent Braille film — carry the translation.

A publisher prints a single run. Their distribution partner attaches the correct language carrier before shipping. Unsold stock gets redirected to a new market simply by swapping the overlay. No reprint. No write-off. No risk.

"One board book. Every child. Every language. At the cost of a sticker sheet."

How It Works

From one print run to every market
in four steps.

🖨️
One print run
The board book is printed in the base language — one production run, one cost, full quality. The printed text remains permanently on the page.
🏷️
Language carriers produced
Low-cost text overlay sets are produced for each target language — stickers, magnetic labels, or transparent Braille film. Minimal additional production cost.
📦
Market-specific fulfilment
Each copy ships with the correct language carrier attached — at the warehouse or by the distributor. The same physical book reaches every market.
🔄
Zero write-offs
Unsold stock is redirected to a different language market simply by swapping the carrier set. No overprint. No remainder tables. No loss.
Publisher Benefits

The economic logic of
one book, many markets.

StoryPatch is not a creative concept — it is a business infrastructure upgrade for children's publishers.

📦 01
Eliminate language-locked inventory risk
One unified stock serves all markets. Unsold copies are never stranded — they are simply rerouted with a new language carrier. Inventory risk collapses to near zero.
💰 02
Reduce localization cost by up to 80%
Full translation reprints are replaced by low-cost overlay sets. A new language market costs a fraction of a new print run — making minority language markets viable for the first time.
🌍 03
First-mover advantage — a protected position
The licence grants exclusive access to a registered, protected technology. No other publisher in your market can operate this system. A legally protected, uncontested category lead.
🧪 04
Test new markets at minimal cost
Launch in Kurdish, Catalan, Vietnamese or any language community without commissioning a separate print run. Validate demand before committing full production capital.
📚 05
Your existing catalogue — instantly multilingual
No new creative development. No new titles. Your existing board book catalogue becomes multilingual from day one. New revenue from titles already in production.
🏛️ 06
Unlock institutional & tender channels
Ministries of education, municipal libraries, UNICEF and UNHCR procurement offices actively seek multilingual early literacy material. Carrier-format books are a direct fit for recurring B2B tenders.
Use Cases

Four markets.
One solution.

StoryPatch addresses four distinct, underserved markets — each large enough to represent a standalone business case, all served by a single publishing infrastructure investment.

🌍 Market #1
Multilingual countries & linguistic minorities
Countries like Germany, Spain, Turkey and Japan host millions of immigrant families raising children in non-majority languages. The 0–3 board book segment offers virtually nothing in their languages — not from lack of audience, but from lack of an economic model. StoryPatch makes every existing title accessible to every linguistic community from one stock.
🇩🇪 Germany · 🇪🇸 Spain · 🇹🇷 Turkey · 🇯🇵 Japan · 🇮🇹 Italy · 🇳🇱 Netherlands · 🇵🇱 Poland · 🇨🇭 Switzerland
👨‍👩‍👧 Market #2
Bilingual & multilingual families
A parent who reads in English while their child follows in Spanish. A grandmother who reads in Turkish while her grandchild hears German. The same physical book — two language layers — one shared reading experience. StoryPatch is the first format built for how multilingual families actually live and read.
Parent reads in one language · child follows in another · same page, same moment
🏫 Market #3
Language education & early literacy tools
The interchangeable text layer transforms a board book into a comparative language learning tool. Children can see, touch and compare two languages on the same page — creating a natural, contextual acquisition experience. A premium product for international schools, bilingual kindergartens and language-learning families worldwide.
International schools · bilingual kindergartens · early language programmes
⬛ Market #4 · First worldwide
Braille overlay — accessible shared reading
A transparent Braille film placed over the original printed page — without damaging or replacing it. A blind parent reads with their fingertips while their child sees the same illustrations. A sighted child reads while their visually impaired sibling follows in Braille. One book. Two readers. One experience. This is the world's first systematised Braille overlay for a mass-market children's board book.
Blind parents · visually impaired family members · shared reading experiences · CSR premium SKU
