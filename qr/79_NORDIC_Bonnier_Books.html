The current problem
Bonnier Books has children's imprints in Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland, Germany, UK and Poland — yet Arabic (200k SE), Somali (100k SE), Turkish (3.5M DE), Polish (900k UK) families are unserved by a single multilingual 0–3 title across the entire group
Bonnier Books is the only publisher operating dedicated children's imprints across all four Nordic countries simultaneously — plus Carlsen Verlag in Germany, Bonnier Books UK, and Bonnier Books Polska. Every one of these markets has a state-mandated early childhood language support framework. Every one of these markets has significant minority-language communities. Not one Bonnier group title is available in a multilingual format for ages 0–3.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
One StoryPatch licence — Bonnier Books deploys carrier sets across all 8 markets from a single group-wide production logic
Pre-printed translation carriers on Bonnier Books 0–3 titles. One Swedish, German or English base — carrier sets for Arabic, Somali, Turkish, Polish, Punjabi, Ukrainian and Sami produced centrally and distributed across Bonnierförlagen (SE), Strawberry Publishing (NO), Carlsen (DE), Bonnier Books UK and Bonnier Books Polska simultaneously. Shared production cost across 8 markets makes per-unit economics the strongest of any publisher in Europe.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One group print run
Any Bonnier Books title printed once in Swedish, German or English — shared base across 8 markets.
STEP 02
Language carriers
AR, SO, TR, PL, PA, UK, Sami… produced once, allocated to each national imprint on demand.
STEP 03
8-country deployment
Each national imprint deploys its relevant carrier sets through existing förskola, barnehage, Kita and nursery channels.
STEP 04
Cross-border exchange
Unsold stock in any Bonnier market redistributed group-wide by swapping carrier sets — zero per-country inventory loss.
