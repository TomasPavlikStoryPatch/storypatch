The current problem
CCPPG publishes 2,000 titles annually in Mandarin — yet China's 55 officially recognised ethnic minorities (Zhuang 18M, Uyghur 12M, Tibetan 7M, Yi 9M, Miao 9M) have no CCPPG multilingual 0–3 board book despite a government mandate requiring one
CCPPG is China's oldest and largest children's publisher, with predecessors dating to 1951. Its Pre-School Pictorial and Babies' Pictorial reach 2M+ families with children 0–7. China's Law on Regional National Autonomy mandates mother-tongue early education for 55 ethnic minorities — a government policy that directly creates provincial procurement budgets for minority-language 0–3 materials. As the state's primary children's publisher, CCPPG is the natural — and expected — institution to deliver this product.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
One Mandarin base edition — removable carrier sets for all 55 minority nationalities, funded by provincial bureaux
Pre-printed translation carriers on CCPPG 0–3 titles. One Mandarin base; carrier sets for Zhuang, Uyghur, Tibetan, Yi, Miao, Mongolian, Korean and more produced centrally — no separate print run per language, no per-language inventory risk. Provincial education bureaux fund and procure the relevant carrier sets for their autonomous regions directly, through CCPPG's existing institutional supply chain.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One CCPPG print run
Any CCPPG board book printed once in Mandarin — the national base for all 55 minority language groups.
STEP 02
Minority-language carriers
Zhuang, Uyghur, Tibetan, Yi, Miao, Mongolian, Korean… produced centrally — no separate print run per language.
STEP 03
Provincial bureau channel
Each autonomous region's education bureau procures and distributes the relevant carrier sets — government-funded.
STEP 04
Redistribution
Unsold base editions redistributed between regions by swapping carrier sets — zero per-language inventory loss.
