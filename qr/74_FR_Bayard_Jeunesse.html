The current problem
Bayard Jeunesse and Milan reach every French crèche and PMI centre — yet Arabic (3M), Portuguese (1.5M) and Berber families in France receive no multilingual 0–3 materials through the institutional channel that serves them best.
Bayard Jeunesse is France's dominant publisher for the early childhood institutional channel — its titles appear in virtually every crèche, PMI (Protection Maternelle et Infantile) centre and Catholic school in France. The group includes Milan (children's magazines and 0–3 board books) and Pomme d'Api. Bayard operates in 10 countries including Belgium, Canada, Brazil, Australia and India. Its institutional reach is unmatched — and its 0–3 output is entirely monolingual despite serving a crèche population where 1 in 4 children speaks a non-French home language.
