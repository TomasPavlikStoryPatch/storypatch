The current problem
58M Spanish speakers, 3.5M Chinese, 2.5M Tagalog — dual-language books are a well-funded education sector with no scalable solution
One in five US children under 5 lives in a household where a language other than English is spoken. Spanish, Chinese, Tagalog, Vietnamese, Arabic, Korean — the home languages of tens of millions of families. Title I and Head Start fund bilingual early materials directly, yet publishers offer no cost-efficient multilingual format.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
Removable text carriers: one print run, every language served
Pre-printed translation carriers placed over the original pages. Publishers produce one English base; language sets for Spanish, Chinese, Vietnamese, Tagalog and Arabic are produced centrally — no inventory risk per language, no new print runs.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One print run
Printed once in English — the base for all community language groups.
STEP 02
Language carriers
Sets for ES, ZH, VI, TL, AR, KO… produced as inserts — fraction of a reprint.
STEP 03
School & library channel
Every school district, Head Start centre and library receives the right language set.
STEP 04
National redistribution
Unsold stock switches language via carrier exchange — redistributable coast to coast.
