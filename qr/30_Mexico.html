The current problem
68 indigenous languages — Náhuatl (1.7M), Maya (850k), Zapotec, Mixtec — government mandate for indigenous-language education with a 0–3 gap StoryPatch can fill
Mexico has 68 nationally recognised indigenous languages spoken by 7.4M people: Náhuatl (1.7M), Maya (850k), Zapotec (800k), Mixtec (500k), Tzeltal (600k). Government mandates indigenous-language instruction — yet the 0–3 gap is completely unaddressed by FCE, Santillana or SM Mexico.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
Removable text carriers: one print run, every language served
Pre-printed translation carriers placed over the original pages. One Spanish base edition; language sets for Náhuatl, Maya, Zapotec, Mixtec and Tzeltal are produced centrally — serving Mexico's indigenous education mandate at a fraction of the cost of separate print runs.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps

STEP 02

CENDI & library channel

Unsold stock redistributed within Latin America from one production run.
