The current problem
G&G (Wien) and Ueberreuter (Berlin) are the same group — yet their combined reach across Austria and Germany serves 560k+ non-German-speaking children 0–3 with zero multilingual titles
The Glöckler-Gruppe unites Austria's largest children's publisher (G&G Verlag, Wien) with Ueberreuter Kinder- und Jugendbuch and Annette Betz (both Berlin). Austria has 350k Turkish, 400k Serbian/Croatian and 130k Romanian speakers; Germany has 3.5M Turkish, 1.4M Arabic and 1.2M Russian. The group's combined reach spans the two most important German-language markets — yet not one title across G&G, Ueberreuter or Annette Betz is available in a multilingual format for ages 0–3.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
One StoryPatch licence — Glöckler-Gruppe deploys carrier sets across Austria and Germany simultaneously
Pre-printed translation carriers on G&G and Ueberreuter 0–3 titles. One German-language base; carrier sets for Turkish, Arabic, Serbian, Romanian and Russian produced centrally — deployed through G&G's Kindergarten channel in Vienna and Ueberreuter's Berlin retail and institutional network in a single coordinated rollout.
