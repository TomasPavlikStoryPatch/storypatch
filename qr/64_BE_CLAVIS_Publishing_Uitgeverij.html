The current problem
Clavis publishes high-quality 0–3 board books in Hasselt and New York — yet Turkish (400k NL), Moroccan Arabic (400k NL), Spanish (58M USA) and Tagalog (2.5M USA) families are unserved by both branches simultaneously
Clavis Uitgeverij (Hasselt, Belgium) and Clavis Publishing (New York) are two branches of the same group — sharing editorial DNA, production infrastructure and distribution philosophy. The Netherlands has 400k Turkish and 400k Moroccan Arabic speakers; the USA has 58M Spanish speakers. Both markets have the same gap: a design-forward independent publisher with a celebrated 0–3 range and zero multilingual titles.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
One StoryPatch licence — two branches, two continents, zero additional print runs
Pre-printed translation carriers placed over Clavis 0–3 titles. One Dutch or English base edition; carrier sets for Turkish, Arabic, Spanish and Tagalog produced centrally. Clavis Uitgeverij deploys Benelux minority sets; Clavis Publishing deploys US sets — from a single shared carrier production logic.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One Clavis print run
Any Clavis board book printed in Dutch or English — the shared base for all language groups.
STEP 02
Language carriers
TR, AR, ES, TL… carrier sets produced centrally — shared across both Clavis branches.
STEP 03
Dual-market channel
Clavis NL deploys Turkish and Arabic sets; Clavis USA deploys Spanish and Tagalog sets.
STEP 04
Stock redistribution
Unsold Benelux stock redirected to US market and vice versa — two-branch carrier exchange.
