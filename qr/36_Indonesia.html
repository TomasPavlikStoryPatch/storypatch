The current problem
700+ local languages — Javanese (98M), Sundanese (40M), Batak, Minang: Bahasa Indonesia is a learned second language for the majority of families
Bahasa Indonesia is the official language but a second language for most of 270M people. Javanese (98M), Sundanese (40M), Batak (8M), Minangkabau (7M) are the real home languages. Government policy promotes local-language early literacy — yet no publisher has a scalable 0–3 format for this diversity.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
Removable text carriers: one print run, every language served
Pre-printed translation carriers placed over the original pages. One Bahasa Indonesia base edition; language sets for Javanese, Sundanese, Batak and Minangkabau are produced centrally — unlocking hundreds of millions of families with a single print run.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
