The current problem
Der Regenbogenfisch is translated into 50+ languages — but each translation is a separate print run with separate inventory risk. Switzerland's 4 national languages and 2.2M expats add domestic multilingual demand that no Nord Süd title currently addresses.
Nord Süd Verlag publishes some of the world's most beloved children's board books — Der Regenbogenfisch (The Rainbow Fish), Weißt du eigentlich, wie lieb ich dich hab? and more. Every foreign-language edition requires a separate print run, separate inventory and separate distribution risk. StoryPatch replaces this with a single base edition and removable carrier sets — across Switzerland's 4 national languages, and across North-South Books' entire US and international distribution network.

Solution · UZ CZ 39118
One base edition — carrier sets replace separate translation print runs across 50+ markets
Pre-printed translation carriers on Nord Süd 0–3 titles. One German base; carrier sets for French, Italian, Romansh, Portuguese, Albanian and Turkish serve Switzerland domestically. North-South Books deploys Spanish, Mandarin and Tagalog carrier sets across the US and English-speaking markets — all from a single shared production logic, eliminating the translation inventory risk that currently runs across 50+ separate editions.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One Nord Süd print run
Der Regenbogenfisch or any Nord Süd board book printed once in German — the base for all language groups.
STEP 02
Language carriers
FR, IT, RM, PT, SQ, TR, ES, ZH… produced centrally — replacing separate translation print runs.
STEP 03
Switzerland + USA channel
Nord Süd deploys DACH + Swiss national language sets; North-South Books deploys US and English-market sets.
STEP 04
Global licensing network
50+ licensing partners receive carrier sets instead of separate print runs — zero translation inventory risk.
