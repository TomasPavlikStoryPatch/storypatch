The current problem
Nathan publishes France's most trusted educational 0–3 titles — yet Arabic (3M), Portuguese (1.5M) and Berber families in France's maternelle and crèche system receive no multilingual materials through the educational channel they rely on most.
Nathan is France's leading educational children's publisher, with a 0–3 range that dominates the école maternelle and crèche educational procurement channel. The Sejer group (formerly part of Editis) includes Pocket Jeunesse and First Editions. Nathan's educational credentials and MEN (Ministère de l'Éducation Nationale) relationships give it direct access to France's 15,000+ crèches and 12,000 écoles maternelles. Its entire 0–3 output is monolingual despite serving an institution where 25%+ of children speak a non-French home language.
