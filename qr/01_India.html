The current problem
22 scheduled languages, 1600+ dialects — yet bilingual board books for 0–3 are effectively absent across all Indian states
Hindi speakers are a minority in ~14 Indian states. Marathi, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, Bengali, Gujarati, Malayalam, Odia, Punjabi — each a market of tens of millions of children. Rapid urbanisation drives first-generation city families to seek bilingual early-childhood books that simply do not exist at scale.
→
Solution · UZ CZ 39118
Removable text carriers: one print run, every language served
Pre-printed translation carriers (stickers, magnetic labels or Braille film) placed over the original pages. Publishers produce one base English or Hindi edition; language sets for every regional market are produced centrally — no inventory risk per language, no new print runs.
How it works  ·  From original to multilingual range in four steps
STEP 01
One print run
Printed once in English or Hindi — the base for all regional language groups.
STEP 02
Regional carriers
Sets for TA, TE, KN, ML, BN, GU, MR… produced cost-effectively as inserts.
STEP 03
State distribution
Each state's bookshops, anganwadis and schools receive the right language set on demand.
STEP 04
Unsold redistribution
Unsold stock switches language via carrier exchange — redistributable pan-India.
